# Student Depression Analysis Dashboard 📊🎓

An interactive Power BI dashboard designed to analyze and visualize various factors affecting student mental health, including academic pressure, financial stress, sleep duration, study hours, and overall life satisfaction.

## 📌 Project Overview
Student mental health is a critical area of focus in modern education. This project leverages data-driven insights to explore how daily habits, academic loads, and financial situations correlate with a student's well-being. By visualizing these metrics, the dashboard aims to make complex student demographic and behavioral data easily digestible for educators, researchers, and policy-makers.

## 📈 Key Metrics & Insights Visualized

The dashboard tracks data for **502 Total Students** and includes the following breakdown:

* **Student Satisfaction by Student Count:** A distribution rating (1–5) showing how satisfied students feel with their current situation.
* **Study Hours by Student Count:** A detailed look at the daily hours students dedicate to studying.
* **Academic Pressure by Student Count (Gender Split):** A stacked bar chart showcasing perceived academic pressure (1-5) segmented by Male and Female students.
* **Financial Stress by Student Count:** A clear overview of the financial burdens students face on a scale of 1 to 5.
* **Sleep Duration by Student Count:** Categorized data showcasing how many hours of sleep students get (e.g., 7-8 hours, More than 8 hours, 5-6 hours, and Less than 5 hours).

### 🔍 Interactive Features
* **Gender Filter:** Slice the entire dashboard by Female or Male demographics.
* **Age Group (Agr_Group) Filter:** Filter data by specific student age groups (A1, A2, A3) to see generational or age-specific trends.

Key Takeaways from the Data
Even Distribution: Factors like financial stress and sleep duration show surprisingly balanced metrics across the student sample size, suggesting widespread pressures.

Academic Pressure: The gender-segmented data allows for deep-dive analysis on whether academic stress impacts genders differently at higher severity levels.
